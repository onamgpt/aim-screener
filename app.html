Learn AIM

What is AIM?

AIM (Automatic Investment Management) is a mechanical rebalancing formula published by Robert Lichello in 1977. The core idea: keep a Portfolio Control value for each stock. When the market value falls well below control, the formula computes an accumulation amount; when it rises well above, it computes a reduction amount. Money moves between the stock and a cash reserve — buying weakness, trimming strength, without predictions.

The pieces

Portfolio ControlAnchor value per stock
SAFE bandDead zone (default 10%)
TriggerMinimum size filter (default 5%)
Cash poolShared reserve for accumulation

This app adds two safety corrections to the original formula: a single shared cash pool across all stocks, and reductions are only computed when price is above your average cost.

When AIM struggles

AIM tends to shine on volatile, sideways stocks. In strong one-way uptrends, buy-and-hold usually wins — AIM trades some upside for steadiness. Always backtest before trusting the method on a stock.
